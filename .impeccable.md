## Design Context

### Users
Professional developers and AI enthusiasts/early adopters who use multiple AI coding agents (Claude Code, Cursor, Windsurf, etc.). They want unified configuration management without juggling JSON files across tools. Context: daily productivity workflows, experimenting with new AI tools, managing MCP servers and portable skills across projects.

### Brand Personality
**Premium** — High quality, polished, trustworthy. Feels like a well-crafted tool worth relying on.
**Friendly** — Human, approachable, not corporate or cold. Welcomes users without being overly casual.

Voice: Clear and direct. Confident without being arrogant. Helpful without being condescending.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Visual Tone**: Dark, sleek, modern developer tool aesthetic. Embraces the dark mode fully—not just inverting a light design, but thoughtfully crafted for dark backgrounds.

**Theme**: Dark mode only. Dark backgrounds (#111111), warm muted grays (#B8B9B6), accent orange (#FF8400) for CTAs and highlights.

**Typography**: Instrument Serif for editorial headlines (adds premium feel), Inter for body text (friendly, readable). Strong contrast between headline sizes (80px hero) and body (14-16px).

**Color Palette**:
- Primary dark: #111111
- Secondary dark: #0C0C0C
- Warm content bg: #F2F3F0 (note: for dark-only, consider dark warm grays instead)
- Muted text: #B8B9B6, #666666
- Accent: #FF8400 (orange)
- Borders/dividers: #CBCCC9
- Card backgrounds: #E7E8E5, white

**Anti-references**: Avoid playful/cartoonish elements. No whimsical illustrations, no casual emoji usage, nothing that feels juvenile or unprofessional.

### Design Principles

1. **Delight through polish** — Smooth animations (700ms ease-out), scroll-triggered reveals, subtle hover states. Micro-interactions that feel satisfying without being distracting.

2. **Premium with warmth** — High-quality visual execution (consistent spacing, crisp typography, refined color balance) but approachable and welcoming, not cold or sterile.

3. **Dark-first aesthetic** — Design for dark backgrounds from the start. Use appropriate contrast ratios, avoid pure white text on pure black, embrace the depth dark interfaces can provide.

4. **Developer-focused clarity** — Information hierarchy matters. Label everything clearly, use consistent spacing (120px page padding), make scanability a priority. These are busy professionals.

5. **Purposeful restraint** — Every element earns its place. No decorative clutter. Clean layouts with generous whitespace. Let the content breathe.
